# Panel

Panels are container elements for grouping related content. They support optional head (with title + actions), body, and foot regions.

## Basic

<Demo>
  <div class="panel">
    <div class="panel__body">Just a body.</div>
  </div>
</Demo>

## With head and actions

<Demo>
  <div class="panel">
    <div class="panel__head">
      <div class="panel__title">Recent activity</div>
      <div class="panel__actions">
        <button class="btn -simple">Refresh</button>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="panel__body">
      <p>Content goes here. The panel head uses flex layout — title grows, actions pinned right.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</Demo>

## Socle head (with tabs or extra content)

The `.-socle` modifier switches the head to a 2-column grid that keeps actions top-right while other children stack below.

<Demo>
  <div class="panel">
    <div class="panel__head -socle">
      <div class="panel__title">Project settings</div>
      <div class="panel__actions">
        <button class="btn -simple">Help</button>
      </div>
      <div class="flair">Additional content can sit here — tabs, flair, description, etc.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="panel__body">Body content.</div>
  </div>
</Demo>

## With shaded foot

<Demo>
  <div class="panel">
    <div class="panel__body">
      <p>Confirmation dialog content.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="panel__foot -shade">
      <button class="btn -blue">Save</button>
      <button class="btn -default">Cancel</button>
    </div>
  </div>
</Demo>

## Linked panel

Wrap a panel in an anchor with the `.panellink` class to turn the whole card into a navigation target.

<Demo>
  <a class="panellink" href="#">
    <div class="panel">
      <div class="panel__body">
        <strong>A clickable panel.</strong>
        <p>Hovering highlights the border.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </a>
</Demo>
